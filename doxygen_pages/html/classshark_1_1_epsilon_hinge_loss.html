Detailed Description

Hinge-loss for large margin regression.

The loss is defined as \(L_i = \sum_j^N \max\{0.0, |f_j(x_i)-y_{i,j}|-\epsilon\} \)
where \( y_i =(y_{i,1},\dots,y_{i_N} \) is the label of dimension N and \( f_j(x_i) \) is the j-th output of the prediction of the model for the ith input. The loss introduces the concept of a margin to regression, that is, points are not punished when they are sufficiently close to the function. Points which are outside of the margin are linearly punished, that is the loss is outlier resistant.

Epsilon describes the size of the margin.

The hinge-loss is not differentiable at the points y_{i,j}+epsilon and y_{i,j}-epsilon.
